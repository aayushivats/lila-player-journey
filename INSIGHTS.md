Actionable Level Design Insights

1. The "Fatal Funnel" River Crossings (Ambrose Valley)
Observation: The "Kill Zones" heatmap shows combat is almost non-existent on 80% of the map, instead concentrating entirely on the two central river bridges.
The Evidence: While "Loot Hotspots" are widely distributed across the NW and SW corners, the "Kill Zones" are hyper-condensed at central chokepoints.
Actionable Item: Level Design should add alternative crossing options (shallow water, ziplines, or a secondary bridge) to redistribute traffic. 
Target Metrics: Mid-game survival rate (increase) and Map Geometry ROI.

2. The "Loot-and-Rush" Pipeline (Grand Rift)
Observation: By cross-referencing Loot, Traffic, and Pathing, a predictable and repetitive "spawn-to-death" pipeline is visible.
The Evidence: On Feb 10/11, the "Paths" view shows players looting at the perimeter (*Engineer's Quarters*), then moving in a perfectly straight line to the central *Mine Pit*, where they consistently die.
Actionable Item: Place high-tier dynamic objectives (e.g., a boss bot or a mid-game supply drop) on the outer edges to break the "B-line" to the center.
Target Metrics: Average Time-to-First-Engagement (increase) and POI "Stay Time."

3. The "Diagonal Dead Zone" (Lockdown)
Observation: Across multiple days, player activity on Lockdown is restricted to a narrow "diagonal corridor" (Bottom-Left to Top-Center).
The Evidence: Heatmaps for Traffic and Kills form a sharp diagonal line. The Top-Left and Bottom-Right quadrants of the map show zero player presence, despite containing significant assets.
Actionable Item: Relocate at least one high-value Loot Hotspot or a primary Extraction Point into the "cold" quadrants to force horizontal rotation and utilize the full map area.
Target Metrics: Overall map utilization and Match Predictability (decrease).
